Executive summary

Session 6 Overview

  • Meeting focused on operational strain, succession execution, leadership development, ERP/AI decisions, sales pipeline quality, and profitability discipline.
  • Arkay remains ahead of prior-year revenue by approximately $6M–$7M, and bookings are about 20% ahead year-over-year, but overtime at approximately $60,000 per week is eroding profitability and exhausting employees.
  • The team agreed that senior leadership must listen more and push ownership down to managers. The next management meeting should give department leaders time to present their challenges, ideas, make-ready improvements, and requested resources.
  • A rolling 48-hour locked production schedule is the central near-term experiment to reduce disruption, waste, and hidden lost hours caused by last-minute schedule changes.
  • ERP, AI, ESKO/Phoenix, AMS, HubSpot, and project management are linked decisions. A smaller decision-focused group should replace or supplement the broad ERP steering meeting.
  • Rates and standards review is a major profitability initiative and will reset machine-rate assumptions for the first time in roughly 20 years, with a September 1 go-live target.
Central tension: Strong revenue and bookings, but ~$60K/week overtime is eroding profitability and exhausting the workforce. Senior leadership must listen more and push ownership to department managers.
